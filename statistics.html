The Relationship Between Residents’ Level of Concern About Rats and the Likelihood of Them Learning About Ways to Control Rat Infestation.

The calculated correlation of approximately 0.35 between the likeliness of someone learning how to control rat infestation in NYC and their level of concern about rats indicates a moderate correlation between the two variables, as shown by the table above. From the pivot table, we found that the majority of respondents who expressed concern about rats are most likely to seek valuable information on how to deal with them. Meanwhile, respondents who fell in the categories of neutral or expressed signs of unconcern tend to lean towards not being interested in learning about methods to handle rats. It could be seen that approximately 78.9% of respondents are interested in learning about solutions to deal with rats, with the majority of the percentage coming from people who expressed a sign of concern about rats. About 21.1% of respondents who either were unsure or not likely to learn ways to control rat infestation came from respondents who expressed less concern about rats (See Figure 1). In conclusion, these results suggest that residents who are concerned about rodents are more likely to challenge themselves to learn about ways to deal with them, unlike people who show signs of unconcernedness.

Meanwhile, roughly 42.2% of respondents are not even willing to join programs that are involved in getting rid of rats. More than one-third of the participants are not willing to join programs that are involved in getting rid of rats, so perhaps respondents may already know about the harmful diseases rats can cause but are unsure about the disease they may transmit.
